Kunalo Funnel

The Kunalo Funnel for software adoption is based on a modification of a marketing funnel. This type of funnel is often used in a marketing automation systems. Although the layers look similar. There are significant differences in how the software adoption funnel operates.

In a typical marketing campaign, the engagement is usually some form of online engagement such as registration to download a whitepaper. In a software adoption funnel, the engagement could be at a hackathon, on the comment section of a code distribution web page, or in a forum. Most significantly, the content used to trigger the download is usually a document that contains code examples.

The ability to provide great code examples on how to use the software is common problem that marketers have. Developers running software adoption campaigns usually run into problems with generating awareness.

What Does Kunalo Mean?

KuNalo - The wisdom of ancient gods applied to developer relations

A thousand years ago, four major gods existed in ancient Hawaii. Ku was the god of war. Kanaloa was the rebellious master of magic that was cast out by the gods. Kane was the god of procreation. Lono was the god of agriculture and music.

While a good developer relations campaign could definitely benefit from all four gods, we decided to create our company name from Ku since he was a fighter and Kanaloa, the rebel.

Developer relations campaigns are often tough because they exist between marketing and engineering development. Sadly, we saw that in many companies, engineering and marketing worked in independent silos, often at war with each other for resources. We chose Ku as the first part of our company name because we wanted to acknowledge the tension that can exist. Rather than one side winning, Ku will help us to join the energies and talent across different groups in an organization.

We took the middle part of KaNALOa as a nod to the need to think about things differently. Developer relations is evolving rapidly with new tools and techniques developed every month. To build successful campaigns, we'll need to learn lessons from the rebels of the world and borrow some magic from the ancient gods.
